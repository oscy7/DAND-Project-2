Introduction¶

In this project, we will be taking a look at a dataset provided by Brazilian hospitals. The dataset contains information of more than a hundred thousand patients and their respective pre-existing conditions, whether they are part of the scholarship, received a text reminder and if they showed up to their appointment or not. The purpose of this project is to see if there are any relationships or insights as to why people may or may not show up to their doctor's apppointment. Specifically, we will be asking the following questions:

1) Does receiving a text reminder help with appointment turn out?

2) Can scholarship status help provide hints as to whether a patient shows up or not.

3) Are older people more likely to show up to an apppointment?

We want to have a clearer understanding in order to see if Brazil can minimize no shows, so we will be diving deeping into these questions by analyzing the data.

Conclusions¶

We can see that receiving an SMS reminder and whether or not a patient was participating in the scholarship program point toward a similar story. In addition, we can see that people who did show up to their appointment are older. A deeper dive into statistical analysis to see if they are statistically significant is necessary.

Limitations¶

There are some limitations of the data. I do think 100k people is a bit low for a sample size of a country of 200 million people. There could be biases in data collection that might cause the data analysis be compromised because of those biases.

In addition, it does seem strange that both scholarship and SMS received collection points returned similar results. This leads me to believe that there are limitations with the way data was collected and represented as just binary 1/0, yes/no.
